Camping

Camping is spending a vacation outdoors, regularly spent in a tent or trailer, but if you're looking for a way to entertain little ones or just want to camp, but not leave home, there's still a solution for you! You can go camping at home! (Though, depending on how you do it, you may have a larger or smaller mess) Here's a few ways you can do that:

  • Put a tent up in your house
  • Put a tent up in your backyard
  • Build a fort and call it a tent and just have a good time
  • Turn off all the lights, use candles and flashlights around your house and sleep in sleeping bags
For real camping, a good location can be hard to find. A lot of factors play into camping. Some people prefer lakes, others don't. It honestly depends on you, but remeber that even if it's not your first choice intially, it may be more fun than you realize. I've done some research and found some fun locations.

Hunting/Fishing

If you're not very comfortable with a gun/bow/knife or sharp hooks & dead animals, then I don't believe this is the activity for you. Hunting is shooting animals, just putting it plainly, hopefully it's for fun and not for survival. Fishing is casting a fishing rod line into the water with a bait and hook and patiently waiting to see if a fish eats it, fish will either be released when caught or kept to take home and eat or disect or whatever. These activites can be very expensive depending on how serious you are about it. Most hunters will own a lot of guns and bullets and different weapons of choice to go hunting. They also have a lot of camo and orange (orange is highly reccomended so you don't shoot your hunting buddy) In order to hunt or fish as an adult, you must get hunting and fishing licenses. As a kid, yes you'll still have to get your hunter's safety and official license, but if you're under the fishing age for your state, you can fish with a licensed adult. Here I've attached a few links to websites that will give you more information and allow you to sign up to take the hunters safety course and where to get your licenses.

Water Activities

Summertime the hottest time of the year, what better way to cool off than to go play and enjoy yourself in the water? Water activites can mean anything from your backyard sprinkler to the ocean. There's so many ways to have fun! We just haven't discovered them all. These are some of the things I've discovered, but if you find more, feel free to use them! After all, curiosity is the best medicine for bordeom. Let's start in the backyard:
  • Fun sprinklers! (just attach attach a sprinkler to a hose and spray yourself and others)
  • Mini pool (If you don't have a pool, find an object you can fill up with water and splash around in it!)
  • Trampoline sprinklers (place sprinklers underneath your trampoline and try to jump around and over them without getting wet!)
I know those aren't a lot of ideas, but you can try them out and see if they're fun. Next we have games in local pools or backyard pools & hot-tubs.
  • Marco-Polo
  • Sharks & Minnows
  • Obstacle courses
  • (Any form) Tag
  • Cannonball contests
  • Underwater handstand & flip contests
  • Pool noodle jousting
  • Pool noodle races
  • Chicken fight
  • Atomic Whirlpool

Winter/Snow Activites

This section is for the people who live in Utah and anywhere else that can brag about their snow. We've got Winter/Snow activites! When it comes to snow, people have a lot of different ways of dealing with it. Some people shovel it and then forget about it. Others don't have to deal with it because they don't have any. Others make it their life to snowboard and ski down mountains that are covered in it. Others make snow men and snow angels. Little children eat it, dogs pee in it. But no matter the case, there's activites for everyone. We'll go down the list from least expensive to most expensive.
  • Snowmen *Free
  • Snow angels *Free
  • Sledding *Free
  • Snow tunnels and forts *Free
  • Snowball fights *Free for the most part (your payment might be a couple snowballs to the face)
  • Snowboarding & Skiing
